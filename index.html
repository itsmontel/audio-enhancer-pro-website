Audio Enhancer FAQ

Is Audio Enhancer Pro really free?
Yes — Audio Enhancer Pro is 100% free with every feature unlocked. There is no subscription, no trial period, and no credit card required. You get the full volume booster, 10-band equalizer, AI noise reduction, and 3D spatial audio at no cost.
What websites does the audio enhancer work on?
Audio Enhancer Pro works on virtually every website that plays audio in Chrome, including YouTube, Netflix, Spotify Web Player, Amazon Prime Video, Twitch, podcast sites, video conferencing platforms like Zoom and Google Meet, and more. The sound enhancer automatically processes audio streams across all open tabs.
How do I boost the volume on my browser beyond 100%?
After installing Audio Enhancer Pro, click the extension icon and use the Volume Boost slider to amplify audio up to 500% — far beyond your browser's default maximum. The intelligent boost algorithm increases volume without distortion or clipping, so quiet videos, podcasts, and music become clearly audible.
Does the sound enhancer extension slow down my browser?
No. Audio Enhancer Pro uses the Web Audio API for efficient real-time processing with minimal CPU usage. It processes audio only on active tabs and is optimized for modern hardware. Most users report zero noticeable performance impact.
What is the 10-band equalizer and how do I use it?
The equalizer lets you adjust 10 frequency bands from 32 Hz to 16 kHz, giving you full control over bass, midrange, and treble. Choose from built-in presets (Rock, Jazz, Podcast, Cinema, Gaming, and more) or create your own custom presets. It's ideal for tailoring audio quality to your headphones, speakers, or content type.
How does AI noise reduction improve audio quality?
The AI enhancement engine analyzes audio in real time to separate speech, music, and background noise. It reduces unwanted noise while preserving voice clarity and musical detail. You can also adjust Audio Clarity and Dynamic Range sliders to balance quiet and loud parts automatically — great for podcasts, lectures, and video calls.
What is 3D spatial audio and how does it work?
3D Spatial Audio uses acoustic modeling and head-related transfer functions (HRTF) to create the illusion of sound coming from all around you, even through regular headphones or laptop speakers. You can select room presets (Tiny Room through Cathedral), adjust spatial width, and optimize for music, podcasts, or movies.
Can I use Audio Enhancer Pro on multiple devices?
Absolutely. Install it on as many Chrome browsers as you like — there are no device limits and no account required. Each installation works independently with its own saved presets and settings.

What Is Audio Enhancer Pro?

Audio Enhancer Pro is a free audio enhancer extension for Google Chrome that gives you complete control over the sound coming from your browser. Whether you are watching YouTube, streaming Netflix, listening to Spotify, or sitting in a Zoom meeting, Audio Enhancer Pro processes audio in real time to deliver louder, clearer, more immersive sound.

Unlike simple volume booster extensions, Audio Enhancer Pro combines four professional-grade tools into one lightweight package:

  • Volume Boost — amplify any website's audio up to 500%, ideal for quiet videos, soft podcasts, and low-volume streams.
  • 10-Band Equalizer — adjust bass, midrange, and treble frequencies with precision. Choose built-in presets for Rock, Jazz, Podcast, Cinema, Gaming, and more, or save your own.
  • AI Noise Reduction & Clarity — intelligent algorithms remove background noise, sharpen dialogue, and balance dynamic range so you never have to strain to hear.
  • 3D Spatial Audio — acoustic room simulation with HRTF processing turns stereo sound into a surround-sound experience through any headphones or speakers.

Audio Enhancer Pro works on every website — YouTube, Netflix, Spotify, Twitch, Amazon Prime Video, Disney+, Google Meet, Zoom, SoundCloud, podcast sites, and thousands more. It is designed for anyone who wants better audio quality from their browser: music lovers, remote workers, students, podcast listeners, gamers, and people with hearing difficulties.

The extension is built on the Web Audio API for efficient, low-latency processing that won't slow down your computer. All audio processing happens locally on your device — nothing is sent to external servers, so your listening is completely private.
